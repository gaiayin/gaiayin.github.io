About Yin Yoga

Yin yoga is a meditative, slow-paced style of yoga that focuses on holding postures for several minutes to target the body’s deep connective tissues, most notably fascia.

Finding Your Edge in Yin Yoga

During yin yoga, yogis find their “edge”, the point in a pose where a significant stretch is felt without any sharp pain. This is the ideal spot to hold a posture for an extended period and deepen the practice safely.

The Physical and Energetic Benefits of Yin Yoga

Unlike dynamic exercises that focus on muscular engagement, yin yoga applies gentle, sustained stress to the body’s deep connective tissues, particularly fascia. This process helps improve flexibility, circulation and tissue health.

Energy and Charge in the Body

Fascia is a web of fibrous connective tissue that surrounds every muscle, bone, organ, and nerve in your body. It not only provides structural support but also plays a crucial role in communication and proprioception.

Traditional Chinese Medicine and Yin Yoga

The principles of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) offer a powerful lens through which to understand the benefits of yin yoga. In TCM, the body’s vital energy, or Qi, flows through channels called meridians — yin yoga can support balanced flow through gentle stimulation of these pathways.

Living in Harmony with our Natural Energy Cycles

Yin yoga supports natural biological cycles, including ultradian rhythms — roughly 90–120 minute cycles of activity and rest. When we push through the natural energy dips we disrupt the body’s ability to repair and restore.

The Difference between Yin and Restorative Yoga

Restorative yoga focuses on complete relaxation and uses props to fully support the body so you can rest without stretching. Yin yoga uses props to help you find your “edge” and apply a sustained stretch to connective tissues.
